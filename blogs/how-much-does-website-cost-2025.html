How Much Does a Website Cost?

August 4, 2025 12 min read Business Strategy

In 2025's digital landscape, the choice between custom-coded websites and site builders represents more than a simple pricing decision—it's a strategic choice that directly impacts business growth, revenue, and long-term success. Our comprehensive research reveals that while site builders appear cheaper initially, custom websites deliver 50-625% revenue increases[1] and typically achieve positive ROI within 6-12 months.

01 Current Pricing Reveals Significant Gaps

The website development market in 2025 shows a clear pricing divide. Custom-coded websites require upfront investments of $10,000-$30,000 for basic business sites and $25,000-$70,000 for e-commerce solutions[2]. Site builders, meanwhile, advertise monthly fees ranging from Wix's $17 Light plan to Shopify's $399 Advanced tier[3].

Hidden costs plague site builder users. Premium apps add $3-$500+ monthly, with the average Shopify store spending $50-$200 monthly on essential apps alone[5]. Migration costs when businesses inevitably outgrow their builders range from $500-$5,000+, and that's just for basic data transfer.

03 Technical Limitations Create Invisible Ceilings

Site builders impose technical SEO restrictions that limit growth potential. Squarespace only allows meta descriptions on main pages, not all pages, violating Google's best practices[10]. Both Wix and Squarespace auto-generate XML sitemaps that cannot be customized, limiting control over how search engines crawl sites.

05 Hidden Costs Compound When Businesses Scale

Perhaps most revealing, 51% of website projects are abandoned before completion, with 91% of users finding some aspect of website building overly difficult[19]. This high abandonment rate foreshadows a larger pattern: businesses typically outgrow site builders within 12-24 months as they scale operations.

06 Future-Ready Websites Demand Advanced Capabilities

The trends shaping 2025 website requirements extend far beyond what site builders can accommodate. 80% of small businesses plan to integrate AI chatbots for customer support by year's end[21]. Dynamic pricing using AI, already adopted by 22% of B2B brands, requires custom implementation[22].

  • AI Integration: Chatbots, dynamic pricing, personalized experiences
  • Mobile-First Design: Progressive Web Apps, app-like interactions
  • Complex Integrations: CRM, inventory, payment processing, automation
  • Enhanced Security: GDPR compliance, custom backup systems

07 Strategic Implications for Business Growth

The research reveals a clear pattern: businesses serious about growth benefit from custom development from the start. The average business that begins with a site builder migrates within 12-24 months, paying 25-40% more for the rebuild than initial custom development would have cost.

Should You Choose Custom Development?

Choose Custom If You:

  • Anticipate significant growth
  • Need custom functionality
  • Operate in competitive markets
  • Value long-term ROI
  • Require specific integrations
  • Want complete control

Consider Site Builders If You:

  • Need a simple brochure site
  • Have very limited budget
  • Don't plan to scale
  • Want to test an idea quickly
  • Have no technical requirements
  • Accept platform limitations

The Bottom Line

Custom websites deliver measurable advantages across every metric that matters. They achieve 30-120% better conversion rates, generate 2-3x higher revenue per visitor, and provide the technical foundation for sustainable growth. The initial investment typically pays for itself within 6-12 months through increased conversions and revenue.
